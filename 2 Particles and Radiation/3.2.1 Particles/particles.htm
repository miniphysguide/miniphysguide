Understanding Physics


PARTICLES MENU



Constituents of the atom...

Shows a simple atomic model and gives information on the proton, neutron and electron. Introduces the atomic mass unit, specific charge, and atomic notation. Also explains what isotopes are and the use of isotopic data.


Stable and unstable nuclei...

Goes over the strong nuclear force, alpha and beta minus decay (and cloud chambers) as well as their equations, and the existance of the neutrino.


Particles, antiparticles and photons...

Introduces antiparticles and compares them with particles. Photon model E = hf is explored as well as interactions of annihilation and pair production and how to calculate the respective photon energies.


Particle interactions...

Explores the four fundamental forces (gravity, electromagnetic, weak nuclear, and strong nuclear), exchange particles and forces between particles, and feynman diagrams (how the interactions are represented).


Classification of particles...

Explains the groupings of particles (hadrons (baryons and mesons) and leptons). Goes over the conservation rules with quantum numbers (including strangeness) and dabbles a little in decay.


Quarks and antiquarks...

Gives knowledge of the properties of quarks and antiquarks and shows you how to work out possible quark combinations for a given particle (e.g. give a possible quark composition of a K0 meson. Briefly looks at neutron decay.


Applications of conservation laws...

Puts the conservation laws onto practice looking at the quark difference in β- and β+ decay. Shows how these laws can be used to predict unknown particles in an interaction, figure out if an interaction is feasible, or figure out what type of interaction it is. Also a small reminder that both energy and momentum are conserved in these interactions.
